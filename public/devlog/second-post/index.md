# Second Devlog Post


Over the last week or so I've been working on a new project, which I've aptly called "Simurgh" - inspired by the mythical all-knowing bird from Persian mythology. 


I had started it with lots of hopes and dreams, but as always these are slowly crushed with reality. Comprehending unreachable moves in chess is quite tough for my 1100 elo self, let alone predicting the complex relationships entailed to it. As a result I've had to cut back some aspects such as castling rights and an passaint (however you spell it). These shouldn't hinder the project in personal terms, but if this were a fully fledged study I'm sure it would hurt more. Along that note - I've thought that this project in particular first the structure of a study/paper rather perfectly, with a clear problem, proposed solution, analysis and answer. I have 0 clue if there is space anywhere to potentially post it, but once I finish I might give transforming my humble markdown into proper latex and posting it in some subreddits. If you - the reader - have any suggestions, I'm all ears! 

In any case, I've been nibbling at the task for a little bit, although its been a hectic week for me. (Moving houses is a logitical nightmare, and yes I spent my week carrying furniture on my bike) So progress has not been as fast as I had hoped, but either way - it's been steady. I've had to rely on AI a bit more with the peturbations themselves, but I'm still maintaining my stance of deep scrutiny of anything it produces. Now that I think about it - formulating my thoughts on AI usage would make a great post, will definitely do that!

So far I feel like my biggest progress has been in my general approach to the problem. Back in the day I would have gone off to the races and started excitedly implementing everything ASAP. But this time I took a slow stroll through a lot of background material and related work. Spent days polishing a proper Readme which served as my marker of direction and source of truth (not to mention its a godsend for AI context) and took just as much time properly developing my data pipeline, trying first on tiny samples, making all the proper graphs and plots, thinking of my splitting strategy ahead of time - the whole lot. I cannot emphasise enough how much headache this has saved me, I can remember just how much of a nightmare this was in my thesis. There I neglected all of these parts and it bit me in the butt BIG TIME. So adressing this makes me really happy. 


I have the data pipeline almost ready (just some more peturbation types to go), so the next step is the model itself. I havent put nearly as much thought to it as the data, but given my data representation my options are really just CNN's or that NN topology from the study that I used in project Zamin. I definitely need to read up on NN design, I feel like I'm lacking there.

So that is what's ahead, for now - cheerio and till next time.

Berim Booooos.
